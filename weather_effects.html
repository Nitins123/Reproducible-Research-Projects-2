The effects of weather events on the United States public heath and economically

Synopsis

The goal of this data analysis is to determine the more impactful types of weather on the United States human population and economically between 1950-2011. I used injuries and fatalities to classify weather events that affected population health most. I used property and crop damage to determine which weather events were most economically impactful.

Data Processing

The storm data is downloadable Here [47Mb] and unzipped with bunzip2.

Convert damage amounts into dollars

convertMagnitude <- function(x) {
  if (x == '') {
    return(0)
  }

  switch(toupper(x),
          '?' = 0,
          '-' = NA,
          '+' = NA,
          'H' = 2,
          'K' = 3,
          'M' = 6,
          'B' = 9,
          x)
}

stormData$PROPDMGEXP <- sapply(as.character(stormData$PROPDMGEXP),
                               convertMagnitude)
stormData$PROPDMGEXP <- as.numeric(stormData$PROPDMGEXP)

stormData$PROPDMG <- stormData$PROPDMG * 10^stormData$PROPDMGEXP

stormData$CROPDMGEXP <- sapply(as.character(stormData$CROPDMGEXP),
                               convertMagnitude)
stormData$CROPDMGEXP <- as.numeric(stormData$CROPDMGEXP)

stormData$CROPDMG <- stormData$CROPDMG * 10^stormData$CROPDMGEXP

Data Analysis

I group the data by weather event types, sum the classifiers (FATALITIES, INJURIES) & (PROPDMG, CROPDMG), and sum the sum of the classifiers which created a total impact for the weather type. I then order the weather types by their total impact descending.

Determine the most impactful storm events on the human population

healthImpactData <- group_by(stormData, EVTYPE) %>%
    summarize(Deaths=sum(FATALITIES), Injuries=sum(INJURIES),
              Total=sum(INJURIES)+sum(FATALITIES)) %>%
    gather(Impact, ImpactTotal, -EVTYPE, -Total) %>%
    arrange(desc(Total))

Results

Impact on human population of the United States

The most impactful weather events that affect population health are tornadoes, wind, heat, flooding, cold weather, and lightning. Tornadoes are a by far the leading weather related cause for injuries and death in the United States from 1950-2011.

# reorder event types for graph order
eventHealthTypes <- reorder(healthImpactData$EVTYPE, -healthImpactData$Total)

ggplot(healthImpactData, aes(x=eventHealthTypes, y=ImpactTotal/10^3,
                             fill=Impact)) +
  geom_bar(position="stack", stat="identity") +
  theme(axis.text.x=element_text(angle=90)) +
  labs(title = "Worst Weather related Fatalities and Injuries in the US 1950-2011",
       x = "Weather Event Type", y = "Total Impact (in thousands)")

Greatest economic consequences

The weather events that caused the greatest economic consequences are hurricanes, cold winter weather, hail, drought, wild fires and tropical storms. Hurricanes are the main weather related cause of economic destruction, predominately property damage. The leading cause of crop damage is drought, but barely compares to the total economic impact by hurricanes.
